Breadboard Computer

This is less of a specific interest but rather a kind of project I see around on the internet that seems like a very good learning opportunity for me. That would be to build what is known as a "breadboard computer". Simply put, it is a computer that is bult from scratch using commercially available electronic components on an electronics breadboard. These homemade computers are usually made with either an 8-bit or 16-bit architecture. What serves as a good learning resource and what really put me on to this project is the excellent ongoing YouTube series by a fellow named Ben Eater in which he creates a 65c02 microprocessor based breadboard computer. ThinkPads

X220

I have a strong interest in ThinkPad laptops. They are my favourite make of laptops, in fact. I like the old ones in particular, because the modern ones seem to falling into some of the pitfalls of modern laptop design. The older models are free from these faults though, and they are tough as nails. Thanks to the easy upgradability of older ThinkPads, I can continue to use my nearly 13 year old laptop well into the future.

To the right is a picture of the very laptop I used to create 95% of this website. This X220 was the first ThinkPad I bought in the summer of 2022, I like it because it has a nice balance of the old-fashioned practicality and aesthetics while also having enough computing power to be perfectly viable for most modern computation tasks. The X220 was intoruced in 2011

T60

I have to confess, one of my favourite types of computer, not just among ThinkPads but in general, are the old 4:3 ThinkPads. I am lucky enough to own a T60 in superb condition, which I bought from a business so the good condition of the laptop is even more notable. The T60 was first introduced in 2006. I swapped the motherboard in mine with that of a T61 which is almost identical excpet that it can accept slightly newer processors.

Unfortunately, it is getting more and more difficult to use the T60 as hardware requirements for popular software and websites is only increasing and even with the best possible hardware, one can expect lag and slowdown, especially for graphical applications. There is, however, a Computer Engineer from Singapore who is currently developing a drop in replacment motherboard for these old laptops which utilizes the latest Intel processors and accomodates current common RAM configurations. It is a small project and if it reaches completion these new motherboards won't be made in large quantities, but it at least gives these laptops a further lease on life.

My Leaving Certificate Project

In LC History, students are required to produce a small body of work on a historical topic which is of interest to them. They are given a wide degree of latitude as to what they would like to do their project on. In my case, I elected to do my project on the first flight from Britain to Ireland as carried out by Denys Corbett Wilson in 1912. I carried out this body of work because aviation and it's history is another interest of mine, and as well it is a piece of local (to me) history that I felt was not properly appreciated. Also, supposedly my Great-Great-Grandfather was in the photograph to the left, taken after the event took place (nobody was injured in the crash-landing, although Wilson later perished three years later in the First World War.)
